History of F1 Races

There are many rules in Formula 1 that each team ("constructor") must abide by. They change every year, but every now and then, the FIA makes significant adjustments to the rules. The 2014 season was one such major overhaul, making it difficult to accurately compare against seasons prior. Our data spans until 2024, so in this application, we analyze a total of a ten year period.

Below is an interactive line chart that displays the cumulative points that each of the top 10 drivers earned throughout a given season. We invite you to play around with our tool! Simply select the season you are interested in exploring and the data for that year will populate automatically. Additionally, you may hover over different data points to get a glimpse of a specific driver's performance throughout that season and race. And finally, to have an easier-to-view experience, you can filter the chart to show only specific drivers by clicking on a driver's name in the legend on the righthand side.


Simulated Outcomes

Our team has developed a way to recompute the championship standings for a given season assuming certain conditions are true, including a "Did Not Finish" (DNF) probability. Using a 10% probability of every driver not finishing in a race, our team has re-run a simulation for each race from 2014 to 2024 and recomputed the championship standings for that season. Additionally, here we introduce our new metric: the Championship Sensitivity Index (CSI). This can be thought of as a measure of how volatile the championship standings were for a given year, assuming the parameters specified were true.
