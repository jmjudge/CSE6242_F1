Formula 1 is often described as the pinnacle of motorsport. It is where 22 of the world's greatest race car drivers compete to become the World Champion. In an F1 season, 11 teams (2 drivers per team) compete in 24 races across 22 countries. It is a multimillion dollar sport organized by the Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile (FIA).

Our tool analyzes how random race events (mechanical failures, crashes, penalties, etc.) affect Formula 1 championship outcomes. Using 75 years of historical race data, we simulate seasons under counterfactual and probabilistic scenarios to recompute standings race by race. By running thousands of simulations, we estimate how often championship results would have changed and produce a quantitative measure of how stable or fragile each title truly was.
